# Product principles

1. **Chat is the control plane, not the archive.** Durable output belongs in Documents, Sources, Claims, Memory, and Research Runs.
2. **Evidence precedes prose.** Factual writing is assembled from claims with inspectable support.
3. **One canonical content model.** Private, public, exported, and model-context views cannot become independently edited copies.
4. **Source versions are immutable.** Mutable external identifiers are never sufficient citation targets.
5. **Simple by default.** Advanced controls appear progressively and remain reversible.
6. **Users can inspect and intervene.** Plans, source scope, progress, conflicts, and patches are visible.
7. **Agents are bounded.** Each worker has a task, tool allowlist, budget, schema, and prohibited actions.
8. **Provider neutrality is architectural.** Models, search engines, parsers, and managed research systems are adapters.
9. **Permissions precede retrieval.** Unauthorized text never reaches a model, reranker, cache, log, or export.
10. **Maintenance is a first-class workflow.** Changed evidence triggers dependency-aware revalidation rather than silent regeneration.
11. **Generated material is derived.** It does not count as independent corroboration of its upstream evidence.
12. **The product reports its real state.** Specifications, partial implementations, and verified capabilities are never conflated.
